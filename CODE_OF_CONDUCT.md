# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Statement on Diveristy, Equity, and Inclusion

In this project we acknowledge and consider perspectives and input from people across all backgrounds, abilities, and perspectives. When we bring together people from different walks of life, we can create a community where people feel empowered to bring their knowledge and ideas to the table. There is no space for hate in this project.

## Expectation of Behaviour

### Acceptable Behaviour

Respectful, professional language is an expectation to all contributors. We want to foster an environment where contributors feel safe to help out, no matter their level. Some examples of encouraged behaviour are:

* Providing *constructive* feedback. Consider using a SMART format: specific, measurable, actionable, relevant, time-bound (this can be optional within reason).
* Using a respectful and professional tone.
* Having productive and thoughful discussions with fellow contributors.

### Unacceptable Behaviour

**Our rule of thumb is that if you wouldn't say it out loud, in person, then you should not be saying it in our project.**

Inappropriate behaviours can include:

* Harassment, bullying, and other derogatory/abusive language.
* Evading a ban by creating an alternate account.
* Spamming pull requests, comments, and issues with unrelated topics.
  * Spamming with related topics isn't good either. If you think something is urgent and *needs* to be looked at immediately, mention one of the reviewers.
* Doxxing. Unless the user consents, NEVER post personal information of a user.
* Plagiarism from other contributors. If you use something that isn't yours, please try to at least link where you took it from and mention who the author is.

Additional behaviours will be inspected under out discretion.

#### Handling Unacceptable Behaviour

If we see any behaviour deemed as unacceptable, or if it's reported to us (use mentions), we will review the issue/comment. If the issue/comment is inappropriate, we have the right to delete or change the content, and possibly issue warnings/bans. We will be operating on a 3-strike basis:

1. **Strike One:** quiet, under-the-table edit of the content. We'll assume it was unintentional.

2. **Strike Two:** editing the content plus a reprimand in the replies. Any future contributions made will be monitored. Good behaviour could mean being moved down a strike level.

3. **Strike Three:** deleting *all* issues/comments made and a ban from the repository. This will also lead to a report within GitHub's report system.
